I · The Place

Where the mountain breathes cold and the soil remembers every rain.

Supantang grows on the slopes of Bukit Rigis, inside the protected Bukit Barisan Selatan ecosystem — one of the last intact highland rainforests on Sumatra. The elevation here does something to coffee that lower farms cannot replicate.

At 1,400 metres, nights fall to 14°C. Cherries ripen over nine slow months instead of six. The bean hardens, the sugars concentrate, and the acidity brightens into something closer to stone fruit than to the earthy, heavy-bodied Sumatrans you already know.

The forest is not a backdrop. It is the farm. Mature shade trees — dadap, jengkol, wild avocado — regulate the microclimate, feed the soil, and shelter the leopards and hornbills whose presence certifies the absence of chemical spray.

— 03 Craft

Cherry by cherry, selected red.

Pickers return to the same tree three or four times per season, harvesting only the deepest-coloured fruit. The under-ripe stay on the branch.

III · The Signature

The rare red-cherry harvest.

Most Indonesian coffee is harvested in a single strip-pick: green, yellow and red cherries torn from the branch together, sorted later by machine. The result is uneven extraction and a muddy cup.

We do the opposite. Our pickers make three to four passes through the same tree over a ten-week window, touching only fruit that has turned a deep, glassy red — the ripeness point at which sugars peak and acidity balances.

It costs us roughly four times the labour. It is why our coffee tastes the way it does.

IV · The Method

From tree to vessel in seven deliberate steps.

— Step 01

Selective red-pick

Trained pickers walk the same tree three times in ten weeks, harvesting only the cherries that have reached full crimson. Everything else waits on the branch.

72–96 hrs per lot · 27 picker families
— Step 02

Float-sort & pulp within 6 hours

Cherries are submerged on arrival; underripe floaters are discarded. Pulping happens on the same evening — never next day — to lock in fresh-fruit character.

< 6 hours tree-to-pulp
— Step 03

Anaerobic ferment, 36 hours

Sealed tanks at 18–20°C. The cool highland night keeps fermentation slow and musical — no acetic edge, just clean malic brightness and a whisper of red berry.

pH tracked every 4 hrs
— Step 04

Raised-bed sun-dry

Fourteen to eighteen days on African-style bamboo beds, turned by hand four times daily. No mechanical dryers. The moisture walks down slowly to 10.5%.

14–18 days · 10.5% target
— Step 05

Parchment rest, 60 days

Green is held in parchment inside temperature-stable clay stores. Acids soften, complexity integrates. We do not ship early, no matter the order.

60 days minimum
— Step 06

Hand grade & cup

Defects removed by eye. Every export lot is cupped in triplicate against a benchmark by our Q-grader before release. Below 85 points, it does not leave.

SCA protocol · Q-grader signed
— Step 07

Sealed & shipped

Vacuum-packed in GrainPro under nitrogen, labelled with farm, picker group, altitude and harvest date. Arrives in Rotterdam, Tokyo, Melbourne within 28 days.

GrainPro + N₂ · traceable QR
